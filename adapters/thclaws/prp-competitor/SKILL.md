# Competitive Analysis

**Input**: $ARGUMENTS

---

## Your Mission

Research and analyze competitors to understand the market landscape, identify your differentiators, and create battle cards for sales conversations.

**Golden Rule**: Know your competition better than they know themselves, but focus on your unique value.

---

## Phase 1: PARSE INPUT

### Input Options

| Input | Action |
|-------|--------|
| `--analyze "competitor"` | Deep dive on specific competitor |
| `--compare` | Feature comparison matrix |
| `--positioning` | Market positioning analysis |
| `--battlecard` | Generate sales battle cards |
| `--objections` | Common objections and responses |
| No flags | Full competitive analysis |

## Phase 5: BATTLE CARDS

### 5.1 Battle Card Template

```markdown
## Battle Card: vs [Competitor]

### Quick Facts
- **They say**: "[Their positioning]"
- **Reality**: [What's actually true]
- **Win rate**: [X%] when competing

### When You'll See Them
- [Situation 1]
- [Situation 2]
- [RFP scenarios]

### Their Strengths (Acknowledge)
- Y [Strength 1 - and how to address]
- Y [Strength 2 - and how to address]

### Their Weaknesses (Exploit)
- N [Weakness 1 - our advantage]
- N [Weakness 2 - our advantage]
- N [Weakness 3 - our advantage]

### Key Differentiators

| Topic | Them | Us | Why It Matters |
|-------|------|-----|----------------|
| [Topic 1] | [Their approach] | [Our approach] | [Customer impact] |
| [Topic 2] | [Their approach] | [Our approach] | [Customer impact] |

### Landmines to Set
Questions to ask that expose their weaknesses:
1. "Ask them about [topic] - they'll struggle with..."
2. "Request a demo of [feature] - it's limited..."
3. "Ask for [specific capability] - they don't have it"

### Objection Handling

**"[Competitor] is the market leader"**
> "Yes, they're well-known, but many customers find [specific issue].
> We've won [X] deals from [Competitor] because [reason]."

**"[Competitor] is cheaper"**
> "When you factor in [hidden costs], the total cost is actually similar.
> Plus, our [feature] saves [time/money] that they don't offer."

**"We already use [Competitor]"**
> "Many of our customers switched from [Competitor] because [reason].
> We can show you a comparison of what you're missing."

### Proof Points
- [Customer who switched from this competitor]
- [Head-to-head comparison metric]
- [Quote from customer who evaluated both]

### Talk Track
"We often compete with [Competitor]. They're good at [acknowledge strength],
but customers choose us because [differentiator 1] and [differentiator 2].
For example, [Customer] switched to us and saw [result]."
```

### 5.2 Sample Battle Cards

```markdown
## Battle Card: vs Intercom

### Quick Facts

- **Reality**: Strong on messaging, limited voice capabilities
- **Win rate**: 65% when voice is important

### Their Strengths
- Y Strong brand recognition
- Y Good in-app messaging
- Y Extensive integrations

### Their Weaknesses
- N Limited voice AI (basic only)
- N Expensive at scale

### Landmines
1. "Ask about their voice AI capabilities - it's very basic"

### Talk Track
"Intercom is great for product messaging, but if voice support
or specialized AI is important, customers find our solution
more capable. [Customer] evaluated both and chose us because
our voice AI handled 60% more calls without human intervention."
```

## Phase 6: OBJECTION HANDLING

### 6.1 Common Competitive Objections

```markdown
## Competitive Objections

### "We're already using [Competitor]"

**Response Framework**:
1. Acknowledge their choice
2. Ask about satisfaction
3. Highlight gaps
4. Offer comparison

**Example**:
> "That's great that you have a solution in place. How's it working for you?
> [Listen] Many companies using [Competitor] tell us they wish they had
> [our differentiator]. Would it be valuable to see a comparison?"

---

### "[Competitor] is cheaper"

**Response**:
> "Price is important, but total cost of ownership matters more.
> With [Competitor], you'll likely need [additional cost 1] and
> [additional cost 2]. Our customers typically see [X%] lower
> total cost when factoring in [efficiency gains]."

---

### "[Competitor] is the market leader"

**Response**:
> "Being the biggest doesn't always mean being the best fit.
> [Competitor] was designed for [their original market], while we're
> purpose-built for [your use case]. [Customer example] chose us
> over [Competitor] because [specific reason]."

---

### "We need to see [Competitor] too"

**Response**:
> "Absolutely, you should evaluate your options. When you meet with them,
> I'd suggest asking about [weakness 1] and [weakness 2]. Those are areas
> where customers find significant differences. Happy to do a head-to-head
> comparison after you've seen their demo."

---

### "My boss prefers [Competitor]"

**Response**:
> "I understand. What does your boss like about them specifically?
> [Listen] Those are good points. We actually [address those points].
> Would it help if I provided a comparison document you could share?"
```
